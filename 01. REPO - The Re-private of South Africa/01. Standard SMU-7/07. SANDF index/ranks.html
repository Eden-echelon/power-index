PRIVATE (PVT)
●
Basic Soldier – Enlisted entry level. Completed basic military training (BMT). Foundation of SANDF ground forces. Core duties: infantry tactics, rifleman qualifications, guard duty, support operations. Promotion after 12 months service or meritorious action. Uniform features single chevron (cloth). Specialty tracks: Armour, Artillery, Air Defence, Engineering. Combat ready – backbone of national defence.
🇿🇦 ENLISTED TIER 1 | BASIC TRAINING GRADUATE
LANCE CORPORAL (LCpl)
●●
Senior Private / Junior NCO candidate. Appointed as fireteam leader or assistant section commander. Demonstrated leadership, weapons proficiency, and tactical awareness. Responsible for section discipline, equipment maintenance, and mentoring new recruits. SANDF standard promotion path from Private after 18–24 months with positive evaluation. Wears two crossed chevrons. Essential tactical link in platoon structure.
🔹 ASSISTANT SECTION COMMAND | NCO TRACK
CORPORAL (CPL)
●●●
Non-Commissioned Officer (NCO). Commands a section (8–12 soldiers). Responsible for combat readiness, fieldcraft training, and administrative tasks. Corporal is the first true NCO rank with authority over junior enlisted. Duties: conducting patrols, weapons inspection, tactical drills. Required to pass leadership development course. SANDF NCO corps essential for unit cohesion and battlefield effectiveness. 3–5 years service typical.
🎯 SECTION COMMANDER | NCO CADRE
SERGEANT (SGT)
●●●●
Senior NCO – Platoon Sergeant role. Oversees 30–40 soldiers, advises Platoon Commander on discipline, training, morale. Enforces military standards, conducts inspections, coordinates logistics. Over 8 years of service or exceptional leadership. Responsible for combat effectiveness of platoon-level assets. Wields disciplinary authority within company. Honorary appointments for meritorious conduct in peacekeeping missions.
📢 PLATOON SERGEANT | TACTICAL EXPERT
STAFF SERGEANT (SSGT)
●●●●●
Administrative & Operations NCO – Company-level. Oversees supply, personnel records, mission planning, and logistics. Often serves as Company Quartermaster Sergeant (CQMS). Manages equipment issuance, vehicle fleets, ammunition stores. Bridge between enlisted soldiers and officers. Requires advanced leadership and technical certification. Key role in maintaining operational readiness for company-sized units (120–150 soldiers).
📦 COMPANY QUARTERMASTER / OPS NCO
WARRANT OFFICER CLASS 2 (WO2)
◉◉◉
Elite technical and tactical advisor. Battalion level specialist. Directly responsible for maintenance standards, weapon systems, communications, and specialty training. WO2’s are masters of their trade – artillery, signals, engineers, infantry tactics. Serves as Regimental Quartermaster or Company Sergeant Major. Minimum 10 years service, completion of Warrant Officer Development Programme. Key authority on discipline and technical procedures.
🔧 BATTALION TECHNICAL AUTHORITY
WARRANT OFFICER CLASS 1 (WO1)
◉◉◉▲
Highest NCO rank in SANDF. Regimental Sergeant Major (RSM) – senior enlisted advisor to the Commanding Officer. Responsible for discipline, morale, customs, and standards of entire regiment/battalion. WO1 often serves as the "voice of the troops." Over 15–20 years exemplary service. Represents the highest level of non-commissioned leadership. Authority second only to commissioned officers within unit ethics and conduct.
🏅 REGIMENTAL SERGEANT MAJOR | ELITE NCO
SECOND LIEUTENANT (2LT)
★
Junior Commissioned Officer – Platoon Commander (30–40 soldiers). Graduate of South African Military Academy or Officer Candidate School. Learns leadership, tactical planning, and administrative duties. Serves under supervision of Lieutenant or Captain. Responsible for platoon welfare, training, and mission execution. Initial officer rank with promotion to Lieutenant after 18–24 months. Golden bar insignia. Cornerstone of officer corps.
🎖️ PLATOON LEADER | OFFICER CANDIDATE PHASE
LIEUTENANT (LT)
★★
Experienced Platoon Commander or Company Second-in-Command. Manages tactical operations, coordinates with NCOs, oversees mission planning. Often serves as reconnaissance platoon leader or specialist unit officer. Manages communication between company command and platoons. Requires advanced infantry/armour tactics course. Responsibilities include personnel evaluations, combat readiness, logistics coordination. Two stars (silver). Prominent junior officer role.
⚔️ COMPANY 2IC | EXECUTION OFFICER
CAPTAIN (CPT)
★★★
Company Commander – Commands 120–150 soldiers. Key operational decision-maker, responsible for training, discipline, combat deployment, and administration of company-level assets. Liaises with battalion staff. May serve as staff officer in operations, intelligence, or logistics. Required to complete Company Commanders’ Course. Holds significant authority for mission success. SANDF captains often deployed in peacekeeping missions (MONUSCO, etc.). Star insignia (three).
🏢 COMPANY COMMANDER | OPERATIONAL LEADER
MAJOR (MAJ)
★★★★
Battalion Staff Officer / Second-in-Command (2IC) of a battalion. Primarily serves as Operations Officer (S3), Intelligence Officer (S2), or Logistics Officer. Manages strategic planning, readiness reporting, and coordination between companies. Plays critical role in command and control. Over 12 years service typical. Major is considered a field-grade officer with influence over doctrine and training. Four-pointed star insignia. Bridge between line companies and higher command.
📊 BATTALION STAFF | EXECUTIVE OFFICER
LIEUTENANT COLONEL (LTCOL)
★★★★★
Commanding Officer (CO) of a battalion or regiment (~600 soldiers). Full command authority – personnel, operations, finance, and strategic direction. Represents unit to brigade command. Manages subordinate majors and company commanders. Must demonstrate outstanding leadership and strategic thinking. Typically 16+ years of commissioned service. Crown or five-point star insignia depending on branch. Decision-making affects national security objectives.
⚡ BATTALION COMMANDER | UNIT CO
COLONEL (COL)
★★★★★★
Brigade Staff / Formation Commander. Serves as Chief of Staff for a brigade, or commands a major training institution. Manages multiple battalions, policy implementation, and strategic development. Interacts with Joint Operations division. Senior advisor to General officers. Colonel is the highest field-grade rank before flag/general officer status. Responsible for large-scale readiness and doctrine. Six-point star insignia. Extensive command experience required.
🎯 BRIGADE CHIEF OF STAFF / SENIOR COMMAND
BRIGADIER GENERAL (BRIG GEN)
★ (gilt) ★
1-Star General Officer. Commands a brigade (3000–5000 troops) or holds senior staff position at division level. Responsible for operational planning, resource allocation, and theatre-level manoeuvres. First rank within the general officer hierarchy. Appointed by the Minister of Defence upon recommendation. Participates in Defence Staff Council. Manages complex multi-battalion operations. Cross-functional authority. Key role in national defence posture.
🌟 BRIGADE COMMANDER | REGIONAL DEFENCE
MAJOR GENERAL (MAJ GEN)
★★
2-Star General – Division Commander or Service Component Deputy. Commands large formations (10,000+ troops). Oversees joint training, strategic logistics, and force generation. Represents SANDF in international military partnerships. Provides expert advice to Lieutenant Generals and the Chief of the SANDF. Required for major peacekeeping force command. Leadership at operational-strategic level. Highly decorated with extensive service record. Two silver stars.
🏛️ DIVISION COMMANDER | JOINT TASK FORCE
LIEUTENANT GENERAL (LT GEN)
★★★
3-Star General – Corps Commander or Deputy Chief of the SANDF. Oversees entire service branches (Army, Air Force, Navy, Military Health). Manages strategic defence planning, national crisis response, and major procurement programmes. Directly reports to the Chief of the SANDF. Holds authority over multiple divisions. Participates in National Defence Force Council. Responsible for long-term militarization and transformation. Prestigious senior leadership.
🔹 SERVICE DEPUTY CHIEF / CORPS COMMANDER
GENERAL (GEN)
★★★★
4-Star General – Chief of the SANDF (C SANDF). Highest uniformed officer in the South African military. Commands all branches: SA Army, SA Air Force, SA Navy, SA Military Health Service. Appointed by the President. Advises the Minister of Defence and Military Veterans. Represents South Africa at international defence summits (NATO partnerships, African Union). Ultimate military authority under the Commander-in-Chief. Responsible for defence strategy, budget, and operational control of 70,000+ active personnel.
🏅 CHIEF OF THE SANDF | HIGHEST UNIFORMED OFFICER
COMMANDER-IN-CHIEF (CINC)
★★★★★★★★
The President of the Republic of South Africa – Supreme Commander of the SANDF under Section 202(1) of the Constitution. Vests ultimate authority over all defence forces: declares war, deploys forces, appoints Chief of the SANDF, approves military budgets, and oversees national security strategy. Authority derived from Defence Act (Act 42 of 2002) and constitutional mandate. CINC holds power during states of emergency, directs strategic nuclear command, and represents the military's highest decision-making body. All generals and officers swear allegiance to the President as CINC. Executive control of joint operations and defence diplomacy. The only rank that can authorize full combat deployment.
