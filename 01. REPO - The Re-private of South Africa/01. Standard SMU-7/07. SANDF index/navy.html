🔱 MARITIME DOMINANCE
🌊 IT ALL STARTS WITH THE OCEAN

The South African Navy defends 3,924 kilometers of coastline, two oceans — the Atlantic and Indian — and vital sea lines of communication. From the icy Benguela current to the warm Agulhas stream, the ocean is both heritage and battlefield. Since 1994, the SA Navy has evolved into a compact but formidable force, projecting power through stealth frigates, silent submarines, and elite maritime reaction squadrons.

MARITIME REACTION SQUADRON (MRS) — THE OCEAN'S ELITE

The Maritime Reaction Squadron is the SA Navy's premier marine-type formation, established in 2006 to replace the former Marine Corps. Based in Simon’s Town, the MRS executes:

  • Visit, Board, Search and Seizure (VBSS) operations
  • Amphibious assault & coastal raiding
  • Force protection & maritime counter-terrorism
  • Humanitarian assistance / disaster relief in coastal regions

The MRS operates with specialized landing craft, rigid inflatable boats (RIBs), and is often integrated with Special Forces Brigade operators. Their motto: "Per Mare, Per Terram".

🛥️ 12 × 11m RHIBs 🔱 300+ marines & operators 🚁 Integrated air support (Oryx / Lynx)
⚓ NAVAL BASES & STRATEGIC ASSETS

Naval Base Simon’s Town: Primary fleet headquarters, submarine base, and dockyard.
Naval Station Durban: Forward operating hub for Indian Ocean deployments.
Naval Station Port Elizabeth: Support for patrol vessels and counter-piracy ops.
SAS Wingfield & SAS Saldanha: Logistics, training, and reserve support.

The SA Navy participates in international exercises like IBSAMAR (with India & Brazil) and Exercise Good Hope (with Germany), reinforcing interoperability in blue-water operations.
